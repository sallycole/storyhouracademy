Unit 1: Single-Sound Consonant Letters

Unit 1 teaches sixteen single-sound consonant letters (b, d, f, h, j, k, l, m, n, p, qu, r, t, v, w, z) and their corresponding sounds. Starting with consonants provides a solid foundation for beginning readers as these letters typically represent one consistent sound.

Unit 2: Consonant Digraphs

Unit 2 teaches six consonant digraphs (ck, ch, ng, sh, th, wh) which are pairs of letters that represent a single sound. After learning single-sound consonants, students are ready to learn consonant letter combinations that create unique sounds not represented by individual letters.

Unit 3: Multi-Letter Vowel Phonograms

Unit 3 teaches nineteen multi-letter vowel phonograms (ai, ay, eigh, ee, igh, oa, au, aw, oi, oy, ui, er, ir, ur, ar, or, wor, ear, our) and their sounds. Learning these common vowel teams helps students decode many frequently used words and introduces the concept that multiple letters can work together to create vowel sounds.

Unit 4 teaches six vowel letters (a, e, i, o, u, y) and the multiple sounds each letter can represent in different words. Understanding that individual vowel letters can make multiple sounds is essential for decoding unfamiliar words and improving reading fluency.

Unit 6: Syllable Types

Unit 6 teaches the six syllable types: closed, open, consonant-le, r-controlled, vowel-consonant-e, and vowel team syllables. Understanding syllable types is critical for decoding multi-syllable words and helps students apply phonics knowledge to longer, more complex words.

Unit 7: The Schwa Sound

Unit 7 teaches about the schwa sound, which is the most common vowel sound in English, and how to identify it in words. Understanding the schwa sound helps students decode and spell words with unstressed syllables, which is crucial for reading and writing fluency.
